Lifes of Bulb

In the light bulb industry, the Average Rated Life (ARL)
is how long it takes for half the light bulbs in a test batch to fail.
It's also been called a half-life.
For example if 100 bulbs are tested and have an ARL of 1000 hours
50 of the bulbs had died when the test time reached 1000 hours.
